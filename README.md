## üîç Key Analytical Findings

### 1. Cancer Risk Factor Analysis
- **Finding:** Obesity showed stronger correlation with cancer history than smoking alone
- **Methodology:** Cross-tabulation analysis with donut chart visualization
- **Insight:** 68% of cancer patients with obesity/overweight status vs. 45% among smokers
- **Evidence-Based Connection:** Research links obesity to insulin resistance, increasing colon cancer risk

### 2. Diabetes & Surgical Outcomes
- **Finding:** Patients with uncontrolled blood sugar underwent 2+ surgeries at 3x higher rates
- **Segmentation:** Classified patients as Normal/Pre-Diabetic/Diabetic based on glucose levels
- **Impact:** Highlights need for better diabetes management protocols

### 3. Healthcare Cost Drivers
- **Primary Cost Factor:** Obesity contributed to 35% higher hospital charges, independent of blood sugar control
- **Age-Based Trend:** Sharp cost increase post-60 years (average 2.5x increase)
- **Regional Variance:** Tier 2 hospitals showed highest per-patient charges
- **Recommendation:** Preventive weight management programs could significantly reduce costs

### 4. Demographic Health Trends
- **Young Adult Obesity:** Unexpected BMI peaks in 25-35 age group signal emerging public health concern
- **Senior Health Patterns:** Post-60 population shows diverging trends (‚Üë blood sugar, ‚Üì BMI)
- **Tier 2 Cities:** Infrastructure gaps correlate with higher treatment costs

## üí° Business Recommendations
1. **Preventive Care Focus:** Implement weight management programs targeting obesity-related disease prevention
2. **Resource Allocation:** Increase diabetes management resources in Tier 2 hospitals
3. **Young Adult Screening:** Early obesity intervention programs for 25-35 demographic
4. **Cost Optimization:** Preventive care investment could reduce surgical and long-term treatment costs
